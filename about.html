Our Team

Margaret Dawkins

Founder and CEO

Meg graduated from North Carolina State University, with a Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering and holds a Master’s Degree in Childhood Behavioral Psychology from the University of Maryland. She is a Board Certified Behavioral Analyst (BCBA) and possesses a Certified Applied Behavioral Analysis Degree.

Meg has over 18 years of experience having worked for one of the largest college preparation companies in the United States for over 10 years (Regional Director for the last 5) and then founding ICP-TEAMS in 2012. Throughout the United States, Meg continues to host workshops for thousands of qualified high school students.

Meg’s passion for helping students led her to create a non-profit to assist under-privileged children. Her efforts included providing tutoring and counseling for gang members to help “get them off the street” and assimilate back into society. She created a scholarship fund to provide an opportunity for impoverished students to attend college. Her ABA Degree provides the skills required to assist in transforming lives of all categories of students down a positive path of education. In 2018, her graduating class placed 95% of their students into the top twenty schools in the United States.

Meg is extremely proud of the 100% Satisfied rating from families who have completed her company’s program. Thanks to Meg’s arduous efforts, she has created a program that is an asset to so many students and families. Over 10 years of research and statistics were conducted and used to create this platform. The result is a program that provides excellent support to students along their journey toward the goal of completing their primary and secondary education. Meg believes in working to provide educational equality for everyone.

Marsha Morgan

President

Marcia Morgan graduated from Texas Wesleyan University with a Bachelor of Arts in Mathematics; she also holds a Master of Science in Secondary Education and a Ph.D. in Research Methods from The Ohio State University. Marcia’s experience as an Educator includes working in the Head Start program and teaching in the Fort Worth public schools, teaching undergraduate courses at The Ohio State University and the University of California at Berkeley and was on the Graduate Faculty at Smith College.

Marcia eventually left teaching and joined the U.S. Army, retiring as a Colonel after 25 years of service. During her career, she commanded units in four different countries, traveled extensively and had two tours at the Pentagon where she worked in support of the senior officers of both the Army Staff and the Joint Chiefs of Staff.

As the President of Educational Management Program (EMP), Marcia combines her passion for students and improving the lives of others with her leadership skills honed through her time in the military.

Teena Clayton

Vice President of Operations

Teena Clayton graduated from Embry Riddle Aeronautical University in 2005 with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Professional Aeronautics. Teena was hired at 19 Federal Aviation Administration (FAA). and spent 38 years as an air traffic controller. She worked in four different locations; Fort Worth ARTCC, Southwest Regional Office, Washington ARTCC, and Eastern Regional Office. She has vast experience in many air traffic control positions; Operations Supervisor, Area Operations Manager, Supervisor Traffic Management Specialist, Manager Plans and Programs, Traffic Management Specialist, Plans and Programs Specialist, Training Specialist and Operations Specialist.

After retiring from the FAA in 2016, she was hired by Scientific Applications International Corporation(SAIC) as the contract Training Manager at Washington ARTCC. She worked for SAIC for 5 years managing 30 employees and an annual budget of almost 2 million. She led a team to rewrite the facility training order to address the most comprehensive change-to-training within the FAA in over 50 years.

As Vice President for Operations, Teena hopes to bring her experience in training students and provide new methods of looking at issues encountered by today’s young minds.

In 1985, Teena was awarded a Medal of Valor from Elizabeth Dole for rescuing an unconscious passenger from a burning vehicle. The vehicle then exploded three different times within minutes of getting him out.

Jeron Robbins

Director of Tutoring

Jeron received his Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering from Utah State University.

Jeron has worked as a Math/Engineer Tutor with ICP-TEAMS for over 6 years. He is also a Mechanical Design Engineer (Composite Industry).

Jeron is a fun-loving, hardworking guy. He passes his tutoring expertise and knowledge of teaching/learning methods to all tutors to create an uplifting atmosphere for EMP students.
